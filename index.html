Camera Resection solver

This Camera Resection solver consists of two C++ classes which calculate camera position, orientation, and pixel scale, given points with known 3d and screen coordinates.

Description

Camera resection is the process of determining the camera location (tx, ty, tz), rotations (rx, ry, rz), and pixel scale (kx), given points with 3d and screen coordinates.

By convention the location coordinates are right handed, with positive x pointing to the right, positive y pointing up, and positive z pointing forward. The rotations are right handed about their respective axes, with a zxy rotate order. So the rotates behave like a standard tripod pad head. Positive rx means tilt up, positive ry means pan left, and positive rz means roll counter-clockwise when viewed from behind the camera.
